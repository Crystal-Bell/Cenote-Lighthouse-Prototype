The **Cenote Core** living prototype is structured as an architectural synthesis of ancient subterranean resilience and modern, autonomous environmental control. By finalizing these specifications, we codify the prototype as a self-regulating, high-permanence dwelling designed for the Jade Avenue site.
### **Technical Specification: Cenote Core Prototype**
| System Component | Specification / Design Logic |
|---|---|
| **Structural Foundation** | Emulates karst-collapse dynamics; integrates deep-pier anchoring to reach stable thermal/water-table layers. |
| **Atmospheric Control** | Multi-level ventilation mimicking "cave-breathing" dynamics; manages humidity and cooling without external power reliance. |
| **Light Modulation** | Strategic aperture placement to replicate "semi-abierto" (partially open) lighting, creating dynamic interior shadow play and natural solar-tracking. |
| **Water Integration** | Closed-loop filtration system referencing anchialine pool chemistry; utilizes natural limestone-bed filtering principles. |
| **Core Materiality** | Hammered/pigmented concrete (limestone-mimetic) combined with organic accents (cedar/stone) for thermal mass and atmospheric grounding. |
### **Integration Protocol**
The dwelling functions as a **Resilience Node** within the broader M.W.S.E. system:
 * **The "Maw" Entrance:** The primary vestibule serves as a transitional space, separating high-friction external environments from the internal sanctuary.
 * **The Vaulted Main:** Centralized communal space under a high-vaulted ceiling, designed to amplify acoustic peace and distribute ambient, low-angle light.
 * **Contemplative Loops:** Tunnel-like passages connect living quarters, providing a deliberate "experiential journey" through the dwelling rather than a standard room-to-room flow.
